# What This Is

The AI Interaction Methodology is a structured approach to working with AI on serious tasks.

It did not start as an attempt to create a methodology. It started as a practical attempt to make AI interaction more usable for real work. Over time, it became clear that the problem was not just prompt quality. The deeper problem was how to make the interaction itself behave more like a controlled system and less like an improvisation.

That is what this is.

It is a way of structuring AI-assisted work so that source authority, reasoning structure, runtime behavior, and collaboration behavior do not blur together once the task becomes complex.

## Why it exists

A lot of AI usage still assumes that better outcomes come mainly from better prompts.

That can help at the margins. It can also be enough for light or disposable tasks.

But once the work becomes more serious, the failure modes change. The problem is no longer just whether the system can produce something plausible. The problem becomes whether the interaction can remain coherent under pressure from ambiguity, iteration, conflicting inputs, shifting goals, and the tendency of systems to drift when their boundaries are not explicit.

At that point, the question is less:

How do I get a better answer?

And more:

* How do I know what is authoritative?
* How is the interaction assembled?
* What defines the reasoning structure?
* What belongs to collaboration behavior rather than reasoning?
* How do I extend the system without quietly redefining it?

This methodology exists to address that layer of the problem.

## What it consists of

The public core is organized around three canonical artifacts.

### Methodology

The methodology defines the runtime contract.

It governs how canonical artifacts are resolved, how configuration is applied, and how the overall interaction is carried from setup to final output.

### Framework

The framework defines the reasoning structure.

It provides the analytical model used to work through tasks in a structured way. It is concerned with the shape of reasoning, not with collaboration style or presentation.

### Guidelines

The collaboration guidelines define behavioral defaults.

They govern how the human and AI system work together during interaction. They shape collaboration behavior without redefining the runtime contract or the framework’s reasoning structure.

These three artifacts are meant to remain distinct. That separation is part of the system, not an organizational preference layered on top of it.

## What it is not

This is not a universal method.

It is not a claim that all AI work should be done this way, and it is not meant to replace expertise, judgment, or direct engagement with source material.

It is also not a prompt collection, a productivity system, or a branding exercise disguised as technical structure.

The point is not to make AI interaction look more elaborate than it is. The point is to make it more stable once the work becomes serious enough that casual prompting stops being sufficient.

## Who it is for

The intended audience is the solo expert operator: someone who already knows how to think through difficult work, but wants a more disciplined way to incorporate AI into that process.

That can include people doing work such as:

* analysis
* architecture
* troubleshooting
* interpretation
* design
* documentation
* structured reasoning across ambiguous systems

It is most useful when the user cares about clarity, consistency, source fidelity, role separation, and preserving a stable foundation while the surrounding system evolves.

## Why the public core is small

The public core is intentionally narrower than the full internal development surface.

It includes the stable canonical foundation and a small supporting layer, but excludes internal roadmaps, conformance machinery, experimental artifacts, and private working material.

That is deliberate.

The point of the public core is not to expose every intermediate step taken during development. It is to present the stable foundation clearly enough that it can be read as a system, rather than as a trail of internal revisions.

## How to approach it

A good reading order is:

1. methodology
2. framework
3. guidelines
4. supporting artifacts, if needed

That order reflects the structure of the system:

* runtime contract first
* reasoning structure second
* behavioral defaults third

## What comes after this

The public core is the foundation, not the full body of work.

From here, other materials can explain how the methodology is applied, where it is useful, where its boundaries are, and how it evolved. But those materials should build on a stable base rather than substituting for one.

That is the purpose of this repository and this document: to make the foundation clear enough that future explanation, use, and development can proceed from something stable.
